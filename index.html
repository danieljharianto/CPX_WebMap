02 · The Envelope

Walls That Let
Heat Win

Many pre-1976 mobile homes in Phoenix have poorly insulated lightweight envelopes that rapidly absorb extreme desert heat, causing dangerous indoor overheating and heavy dependence on air conditioning for basic thermal survival. On a 115°F Phoenix afternoon, interior walls reach 95°F before noon.

03 · The Burden

Cooling Bills
as Debt Traps

Mobile homes in Phoenix consume 20–30% more energy than comparable site-built housing, forcing many low-income residents to spend a disproportionate share of their income on cooling costs as a structural “heat tax”. Residents earning below median income spend up to 42 cents of every dollar on summer electricity bills imposed by building quality, not choice.

04 · The Opportunity

Retrofits That
Pay Back

Shallow retrofit strategies such as shading, HVAC upgrades, and LED lighting can reduce cooling demand by 28–35%, while deeper retrofits integrating roof insulation and solar PV systems may achieve up to 45% EUI reduction with long-term energy savings over time, with payback under 7 years.
